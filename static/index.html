Functions

The 8 Cognitive Functions

The 8 Cognitive Functions is a breakdown of your typical thought process.   Learn more ▼

There are 2 things the brain can do:

  • perceive: take in information. You can perceive by

    • Sensing: take in details
    • Intuiting: take in the overall situation

  • judge: decide how to act, think, or feel given that information. You can judge by

    • Thinking: Make decisions objectively
    • Feeling: Make decisions emotionally

Each of these can also be directed either internally or externally. So for example, the a Thinking function that's directed externally is called "Extraverted Thinking (Te)," and is a type of Judging.

This gives a total of 8 functions. Different personalities prefer to use different functions more than others.
